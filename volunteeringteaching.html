Teaching

Engineering Education for Middle School, High School, and Undergraduate Students in Underrepresented Communities and Developing Nations

I worked with E.L.i.T.E. based in Harlem, NYC and Global Code based in Accra, Ghana to develop curriculum, structure learning, assist in hands-on material, and teach classroom lectures. Depending on the level of education (Middle/High/Undergraduate), the engineering material was tailored to beginner, intermediate, or advanced exposure. As an example, beginner level would be exposed to engineering principles integrated math, physics, and applied technology to create basic programs teaching students to translate their coursework to real life user experience. The advanced level would involve creating applications to provide solutions for real world problems often programming Raspberry Pis/Arduinos paired with circuitry/robotics and larger engineered prototypes.

University Teaching Assistant

During my graduate studies, I served as a teaching assistant for a graduate genomics course. This involved teaching lectures, grading papers and assignments, hosting office hours, and providing access to discuss interests in the field of genomics. I also had the opportunity to curate and lead several workshops such as an annual data reproducibility bootcamp focused on providing hands-on guidance and material in fundamental/advanced techniques to improve productivity, rigor, and reproducibility in software/analytic development in the research and development setting.

Outreach and Mentorship

Mentorship for Aspiring/Interested STEM Professionals

As a volunteer mentor, I guided middle school, high school, and undergraduate students through their professional development in pursuit of higher education. I participated in several programs such as the Letters to a Pre-Scientist (highly recommend) and CUNY mentorship programs. I believe its important to share lessons learned through your path to help others seeking advice or displaying interest.
